01 - Baltimore cafe's app

Project introduction

For this project my role was to lead the app and do the UX Design.

The goal of this project was to design an app to allow customers to order food menu items online before going to get them at the place.

Considering the target audience of customers, we needed to make the ordering process easy to understand.

We also needed to make the process fast because people didn't had much time.

Research study details

Our reseach questions were :

  • "How long does it take a user to order food menu items in the app?"
  • "What can we learn from the steps that users take to order food menu items"

For the study we had 5 participants : 2 males, 2 females and 1 non-binary individual who were between the age of 18 and 50.

The methodology was the following : 5 minutes per participant, at Baltimore or remote, with an unmoderated usability study. Users were asked to follow a prompt with a low fidelity prototype.

Wireframes

User testing result

The following themes emerged from the user testing :

  • The cart is not visible enough
  • There is no items suggestions
  • Not enough feedback

To correct those points we added more feedback via notifications and suggestions based on user previous items selected.

Conclusions

Even though the app had a small number of pages, making an intuitive navigation was not that simple.

User feedback was a key to understand that we needed more feedback on the app, and this feedback made the app more easy to use.
